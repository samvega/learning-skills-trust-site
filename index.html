What we do

A modern measure of a child's education

The Learning Skills Trust is the umbrella charity behind two connected frameworks — giving schools a clear, consistent way to develop and track the things that shape confident young adults.

The PSB

Our flagship framework for Reception to Year 8 — developing and celebrating the skills, attitudes and behaviours children need to thrive at their next school.

Explore the PSB →

The SDF

Our Skills Development Framework extends the same philosophy up to Year 11 — a modern pilot built for the demands of secondary education and beyond.

Explore the SDF →

A shared community

Membership connects a network of schools, teachers and leaders — with moderation meetings, shared resources and an annual conference.

The building blocks

Six skills for a lifetime

Every framework we build turns around the same six core skills — the foundations of a confident, capable young person.

01

Thinking

Critical, creative and independent thought — the ability to reason, question and solve.

02

Communication

Expressing ideas with clarity and confidence, and listening as well as speaking.

03

Collaboration

Working with others toward a shared goal — the teamwork real life depends on.

04

Leadership

Taking initiative, showing integrity and bringing others with you.

05

Independence

Self-direction, resilience and ownership of one's own learning.

06

Review

Reflecting honestly, evaluating progress and always looking to improve.

Skills for a changing world

The gap that grades leave behind

A content-heavy curriculum tells you what a child knows. It says far less about how they think, collaborate, adapt and lead. That gap only widens as the world changes faster.

  • Develop flexible, adaptable intelligence, not just recall
  • Make character, communication and teamwork visible and valued
  • Give pupils — and parents — a fuller picture of real progress
